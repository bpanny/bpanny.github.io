About Me

Welcome to my homepage! My name is Ben and I'm currently a PhD student in the Intelligent Systems Program at the University of Pittsburgh School of Computing and Information. I previously completed my Master's in Biostatistics from the University of Pittsburgh School of Public Health, where I wrote my thesis on symptom dynamics in cancer patients undergoing chemotherapy treatment.

Scientific Research

My research aim is to leverage computational, statistical, and mathematical techniques to improve public health. My current research projects ask the following questions:

  • Why are vaccination rates either below or falling toward the threshold needed for herd immunity?
  • What makes vaccine related content go viral?
  • How does pro- and anti-vaccine information change people's vaccine beliefs and behaviors? What is the cost of these changes in terms of limited vaccine uptake and subsequent infectious disease transmission?
  • What is the cost-effectiveness of software-based interventions for psychiatric disorders compared to pharmaceutical interventions or the combination of the two?
  • How can we automate the time-intensive process of building models for decision-making in healthcare?
  • How can we optimize dynamic social network algorithms to run efficiently on high-performance computing hardware?

You can find a full list of my scientific publications on my Google Scholar Page.
